Kenny Lau

I am a Physics PhD candidate working on experimental Cosmology at the University of Minnesota. My research focuses on the measurement of degree-scale B-mode polarization in the Cosmic Microwave Background (CMB), and I am working in the BICEP/Keck Collaboration and CMB-S4 project for this scientific mission.

My collaboration is running BICEP3 and BICEP Array, two small aperture telescopes relentlessly scanning ~1.5% of the sky at the Amundsen-Scott South Pole station, for high precision and multiple frequency CMB polarization maps to search for inflation signals. I participate in the instrument development, operation and data analysis of these telescopes.
